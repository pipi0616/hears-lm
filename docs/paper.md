# A hearing-loss–specific protein language model learns transferable variant biophysics, aligns with expert clinical decisions, and prospectively predicts ClinVar reclassifications

---

## Abstract

Hereditary hearing loss is among the most genetically heterogeneous Mendelian disorders, with pathogenic variants distributed across more than 150 genes encoding ion channels, motor proteins, and structural components of the cochlear epithelium. Clinical sequencing routinely returns missense variants of uncertain significance, and although general-purpose predictors such as AlphaMissense have improved per-variant scoring, none has been adapted to the biology distinguishing hearing-related proteins from the broader proteome. We introduce HEARS-LM, a domain-adapted protein language model that couples ESM-2 sequence embeddings refined through low-rank adaptation with one hundred curated hearing-loss–relevant biological descriptors through cross-attention fusion, and trains four task-specific heads jointly under a thermodynamic auxiliary objective supervised by FoldX folding free-energy estimates. On thirty-six hearing-loss genes, HEARS-LM produces per-residue disease maps that, in paired bootstrap comparisons on the twenty-six genes with sufficient ClinVar density, outperform AlphaMissense on twelve genes and ESM-2 zero-shot on twenty-two, with no significant losses. The auxiliary thermodynamic head generalizes to ten genes never seen during folding-energy supervision (aggregate Spearman ρ = 0.817; per-gene median 0.826), and its high-confidence predictions in voltage-gated potassium channels recover known transmembrane-pore disease hotspots through three-dimensional clustering. Predictions agree with PP3 and BP4 evidence codes assigned by the ClinGen Hearing Loss Variant Curation Expert Panel (Mann–Whitney p = 1.2 × 10⁻⁶ and 8.4 × 10⁻⁷), match expert classifications on sixty-six held-out variants with AUROC 0.901, and anticipate ClinVar pathogenicity upgrades sixteen months ahead of expert reclassification. We release a thirty-six–gene per-residue tolerance atlas, a catalogue of twenty ultra-novel candidate positions, and an OTOF candidate map relevant to the active DB-OTO gene-therapy programme.

## Introduction

Hereditary hearing loss (HHL) affects approximately one in five hundred newborns and is the most common Mendelian sensory disorder of childhood (Korver et al., 2017; Morton & Nance, 2006). More than 150 genes underlie monogenic hearing loss, and the resulting locus heterogeneity has driven adoption of exome and gene-panel sequencing in audiologic and otolaryngologic clinics (Sloan-Heggen et al., 2016; Bademci et al., 2016; Shearer & Smith, 2015). Diagnostic yield is bounded by a computational bottleneck: a growing fraction of returned reports list variants of uncertain significance (VUS) whose biological consequence cannot be confidently determined (Richards et al., 2015; Oza et al., 2018). ClinVar contains more than thirty thousand missense VUS distributed across HHL-associated genes, only a small fraction of which receive expert curation in any year (Landrum et al., 2020).

The dominant strategy for triaging VUS at scale is computational variant-effect prediction. Two design families have emerged. The first, exemplified by AlphaMissense (Cheng et al., 2023), uses AlphaFold-derived structural context (Jumper et al., 2021) together with self-supervised sequence pretraining to assign a proteome-wide pathogenicity probability. The second, including REVEL (Ioannidis et al., 2016), VEST4 (Carter et al., 2013), and METARNN (Li et al., 2022), combines many other predictor scores through supervised meta-learning, achieving high retrospective AUROC at the cost of dependence on the upstream score pipeline. A third route derives variant scores directly from protein language models such as ESM-2 (Lin et al., 2023) via the masked-marginal log-likelihood ratio between reference and variant amino acid (Meier et al., 2021), an approach that has shown strong zero-shot generalization on deep mutational scanning benchmarks (Riesselman et al., 2018; Frazer et al., 2021).

None of these leading predictors is adapted to the biology of the hearing system. The inner-ear proteome is concentrated in transmembrane potassium channels, unconventional myosins, large extracellular tectins, and atypical adhesion molecules whose pathogenic variants cluster in specific functional domains rather than distributing uniformly along the sequence (Sineni et al., 2019; Booth et al., 2018). Proteome-wide predictors are largely insensitive to these gene- and domain-specific concentrations of pathology. They are also insensitive to allele-frequency context, even though gnomAD-derived population frequency is among the strongest single signals for benign classification in HHL practice (Karczewski et al., 2020): an HHL-aware classifier using only allele frequency reaches AUROC > 0.94 on common ClinVar held-out splits, indicating that much of the apparent performance of general predictors on such splits reflects access to frequency information rather than learned protein biology.

Two technical developments suggest a path forward. First, low-rank adaptation (Hu et al., 2022) enables specialization of a large pretrained network with a small number of additional adapter weights, mitigating catastrophic forgetting. Second, multi-task learning with auxiliary biophysical supervision—predicting folding free energies alongside clinical labels—constrains neural representations toward physically meaningful axes of variation (Tollefson et al., 2024; Diaz et al., 2024). Combining these ingredients within an HHL-specific feature context offers a principled route to a predictor that learns hearing-system protein biology rather than only memorizing per-variant labels.

We describe such a system here. HEARS-LM (Hereditary EAR Sequence Language Model) couples the thirty-layer, one-hundred-fifty-million-parameter ESM-2 variant (Lin et al., 2023), specialized through low-rank adaptation, with a curated panel of one hundred HHL-relevant biological descriptors via cross-attention fusion. Four task-specific heads are trained jointly. We show that the model learns transferable variant biophysics that generalizes to unseen HHL genes, produces per-residue disease maps that significantly outperform AlphaMissense on the most clinically actionable syndromic genes, recovers known disease hotspots through three-dimensional spatial clustering, aligns with ClinGen expert evidence-code assignments at p < 10⁻⁶, and prospectively predicts ClinVar reclassifications more than a year before they are issued. The model is accompanied by three clinical resources: a thirty-six–gene per-residue tolerance atlas, a high-confidence VUS reclassification catalogue, and an OTOF candidate map directly relevant to the active DB-OTO gene therapy programme.

## Methods

### Variant cohorts and data partitions

We assembled five non-overlapping variant cohorts from ClinVar (Landrum et al., 2020), the Deafness Variation Database (DVD; Azaiez et al., 2018), and large-scale multiplexed assay of variant effect (MAVE) datasets aggregated through MaveDB (Esposito et al., 2019). The training set comprised 36,397 missense variants in 112 HHL-associated genes. Three held-out evaluation sets were curated to dissect distinct generalization regimes. The first, an allele-frequency–independent set (n = 2,835; 73 % pathogenic) was constructed from DVD-curated variants whose gnomAD population maximum allele frequency was zero, eliminating the confounding influence of allele frequency on apparent performance. The second, a temporal hold-out (n = 1,885; 47 % pathogenic), comprised ClinVar variants whose first curation date was on or after January 1, 2024. The third, a gene-disjoint hold-out (n = 1,004; 73 % pathogenic), was constructed by withholding twenty-seven genes entirely from training. A separate functional benchmark (n = 17,367) consisting of MAVE-derived fitness scores from auditory-relevant genes was reserved exclusively for ancillary calibration analysis. The thermodynamic supervision target was the per-variant folding free-energy change (ΔΔG) computed by the FoldX force field (Schymkowitz et al., 2005) on AlphaFold2 monomer structures (Jumper et al., 2021), following the protocol established for hearing-loss variant analysis by Tollefson et al. (2024). Targets were available for 112 of the 122 training-relevant HHL genes; the remaining ten genes were withheld in their entirety from thermodynamic supervision to support the held-out generalization analysis described below.

### Feature engineering

For every variant we computed one hundred HHL-relevant biological descriptors, grouped into ten categories. Cell-type expression features (n = 44) encoded protein abundance across cochlear hair cells, supporting cells, and neural lineages from published single-cell atlases (Kolla et al., 2020). Allele-frequency features (n = 10) summarized gnomAD population statistics, including subcontinental frequencies and constraint scores (Karczewski et al., 2020). Complex API features (n = 9) captured protein-protein interaction context. Mouse-phenotype features (n = 8) were derived from IMPC hearing-related ontology terms (Bowl et al., 2017). HPO disease features (n = 6) summarized human phenotype ontology annotations specific to hearing impairment (Köhler et al., 2021). Five additional small groups encoded CGD inheritance modality (Solomon et al., 2013), structural-domain context, evolutionary conservation, gnomAD constraint, and metadata. A full feature schema with dictionary references is provided in Supplementary Table S5.

### Model architecture

HEARS-LM consists of three modular stages: a pretrained sequence encoder, a feature-fusion module, and a multi-task output head bank.

The sequence encoder is the thirty-layer, one-hundred-fifty-million-parameter variant of ESM-2 (Lin et al., 2023), whose self-supervised pretraining on the UniRef50 protein universe produces residue-level embeddings that capture evolutionary and structural context. To adapt these embeddings to HHL biology without overwriting the original weights, we insert low-rank adapters (Hu et al., 2022) of rank sixteen into the query and value projection matrices of each attention sub-block. Only the adapter weights are updated during training, preserving the broad evolutionary representations learned during pretraining while permitting hearing-loss–specific refinement. Given a wild-type and mutant amino-acid pair at residue $i$ in a protein of length $L$, the encoder produces a pair of contextual embeddings $h_i^{\text{wt}}, h_i^{\text{mut}} \in \mathbb{R}^{d}$, where $d = 640$. For variants near sequence termini we apply a sliding-window crop of width 1,024 amino acids centered on the variant residue, preserving full context for the modal HHL protein length.

The feature-fusion module aligns the variant-centric difference embedding $\Delta h_i = h_i^{\text{mut}} - h_i^{\text{wt}}$ with the curated one-hundred-dimensional HHL feature vector $\mathbf{x}_{\text{HHL}}$ through a cross-attention mechanism with $H = 4$ attention heads:

$$
\mathbf{q}_h = W_q^{(h)} \Delta h_i, \quad \mathbf{k}_h = W_k^{(h)} \mathbf{x}_{\text{HHL}}, \quad \mathbf{v}_h = W_v^{(h)} \mathbf{x}_{\text{HHL}},
$$

$$
\mathbf{z}_h = \mathrm{softmax}\!\left(\frac{\mathbf{q}_h \mathbf{k}_h^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)\mathbf{v}_h, \quad \mathbf{z} = W_o \, [\mathbf{z}_1 \,\Vert\, \cdots \,\Vert\, \mathbf{z}_H],
$$

where $\Vert$ denotes concatenation and $d_k = d / H$. The fused representation $\mathbf{z}$ then feeds four task-specific heads: a binary pathogenicity classifier with sigmoid output, a thermodynamic regressor with linear output, a five-way mechanism classifier (loss-of-function, gain-of-function, dominant-negative, trafficking, structural) with softmax output, and an ordinal severity head producing scores on a four-level scale. Each head is a two-layer perceptron with hidden width 256 and GELU activations. We initialize the LoRA adapter weights such that $\mathbf{B} = 0$, ensuring that at training step zero the model is functionally equivalent to ESM-2 with random task heads, and we apply layer normalization to the fused representation before each head to stabilize the multi-task gradient flow.

### Training objective

The pathogenicity head is supervised with the focal binary cross-entropy loss (Lin et al., 2017), which down-weights well-classified examples and mitigates the strong class imbalance between pathogenic and benign variants in the training corpus:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{path}} = -\,\alpha_{t} \,(1 - p_{t})^{\gamma} \log p_{t},
$$

where $p_t = p$ if the variant is pathogenic and $p_t = 1 - p$ otherwise, $\gamma = 2.0$ is the focusing parameter, and $\alpha_t$ is a class-balancing weight derived from the empirical pathogenic fraction. The thermodynamic head minimizes the mean squared error against FoldX ΔΔG targets, evaluated only on variants for which the target is defined. The mechanism head minimizes multi-class cross-entropy. The severity head minimizes the standard ordinal cross-entropy using cumulative-logit parameterization (Cao et al., 2020). The joint objective is the weighted sum

$$
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{path}} + 0.30 \, \mathcal{L}_{\text{ΔΔG}} + 0.20 \, \mathcal{L}_{\text{mech}} + 0.15 \, \mathcal{L}_{\text{sev}},
$$

with weights selected by held-out grid search such that pathogenicity remains the dominant objective while the auxiliary tasks meaningfully shape the shared representation (Figure 1d). Optimization used the AdamW optimizer (Loshchilov & Hutter, 2019) with a peak learning rate of 1 × 10⁻⁴ on the adapter and head parameters, cosine decay, a 500-step linear warm-up, weight decay of 0.01, gradient clipping at unit ℓ²-norm, three training epochs, and a batch size of thirty-two on a single A100 graphics processor. Mixed-precision arithmetic in 16-bit brain floating-point format approximately halved training time without measurable loss in held-out performance.

### Inference and per-residue tolerance landscapes

At inference, each variant is scored independently. To construct per-residue tolerance maps for downstream clinical analysis, we computed the mean predicted pathogenicity over all nineteen non-reference amino-acid substitutions at every position of each evaluated gene, yielding a per-residue saturation profile. Per-residue receiver operating characteristic curves were constructed by treating residues at which at least one ClinVar pathogenic or likely-pathogenic variant has been deposited as positive labels and the remaining residues as negative labels. Precision-at-K was defined as the fraction of the top-K predicted residues that carry at least one confirmed pathogenic variant.

### Statistical analyses

Comparisons between HEARS-LM and individual baseline predictors used paired bootstrap resampling with 1,000 iterations, reporting median ΔAUROC and 95 % confidence intervals. Per-gene comparisons applied the same procedure stratified by gene. Differences in mean predicted score between variant subsets were assessed by two-sided Mann–Whitney U tests. Enrichments of high-confidence pathogenic predictions in specific protein domains were tested by the hypergeometric distribution against a uniform residue-distribution null. Three-dimensional spatial clustering was assessed by computing the mean pairwise Cα distance among high-confidence pathogenic positions on AlphaFold structures and comparing this statistic to a permutation null in which the same number of residues were sampled uniformly without replacement across 10,000 permutations. The prospective ClinVar reclassification analysis used a snapshot of ClinVar curated on January 1, 2025 to identify variants then classified as uncertain significance, and a subsequent snapshot from April 1, 2026 to identify which variants had been reclassified to pathogenic in the intervening sixteen months. Multiple-comparison correction across the thirty-six-gene per-residue landscape used Benjamini–Hochberg false discovery rate control at q = 0.05.

### Baseline predictors

We compared HEARS-LM against ten reference predictors that together span the major design paradigms in the field. AlphaMissense (Cheng et al., 2023) provides a transformer-based proteome-wide score that integrates AlphaFold structural context with self-supervised sequence pretraining. ESM-2 zero-shot scores were computed as the masked-marginal log-likelihood ratio between the variant and reference amino acid (Meier et al., 2021), using the same thirty-layer model that anchors HEARS-LM but without any task-specific adaptation, providing a clean test of what specialization contributes. ESM-1b zero-shot scores (Brandes et al., 2023) were computed analogously. REVEL (Ioannidis et al., 2016), VEST4 (Carter et al., 2013), MutPred2 (Pejaver et al., 2020), PolyPhen-2 (Adzhubei et al., 2010), and PrimateAI (Sundaram et al., 2018) span the supervised-classifier family. METARNN (Li et al., 2022) and BAYESDEL with allele-frequency support (Feng, 2017) represent meta-stacking predictors that combine more than twenty upstream variant-effect scores. For each predictor we used the version and score interpretation recommended by its developers. For predictors with directional sign conventions (such as the ESM zero-shot log-likelihood ratio, for which more negative values denote greater predicted impact) we negated the score before AUROC computation so that all predictors share the convention that larger values denote higher predicted pathogenicity.

## Results

Our central question is whether biology-aware specialization, rather than per-variant score maximization, is the more productive target for hearing-loss variant interpretation. We address it through a cumulative line of evidence that establishes the model's performance on the conventional pathogenicity task, demonstrates that its auxiliary thermodynamic head generalizes to genes never seen during supervision, applies that internalized biophysics to per-residue tolerance landscapes, recovers established structural-functional disease signatures in voltage-gated channels, aligns with ClinGen expert classifications and prospectively predicts ClinVar reclassifications, and finally produces an OTOF-specific resource directly relevant to the active DB-OTO gene-therapy programme.

### A defensible per-variant baseline without meta-stacking

On the allele-frequency–independent benchmark of 2,835 variants in which gnomAD population frequency was zero by construction (pathogenic rate 73 %), HEARS-LM attained AUROC 0.840 (Figure 1f, Table 1), against 0.661 for the same ESM-2 backbone queried zero-shot through masked-marginal log-likelihood (Meier et al., 2021) and 0.764 for a gradient-boosted classifier trained on the curated feature panel without sequence information. The thirteen-point margin over the feature-only baseline measures the contribution of the protein language model; the eighteen-point margin over zero-shot measures the contribution of domain specialization. On the temporal hold-out (ClinVar curations from January 2024 onward), HEARS-LM attained 0.973; on the gene-disjoint hold-out withholding twenty-seven genes from training, 0.945 (Table 1). These splits should be interpreted with care: allele frequency alone reaches AUROC > 0.94 on both, and much of the cross-predictor variation here reflects access to population context rather than learned biology (Karczewski et al., 2020). We therefore designate the allele-frequency–independent set as the principal per-variant test. Two meta-stackers (METARNN at 0.898; BAYESDEL with allele-frequency support at 0.869) achieved marginally higher AUROC on that set, but both depend on more than twenty upstream variant-effect scores—a design that improves the headline number while restricting coverage to variants whose upstream predictors have all returned scores (Li et al., 2022; Feng, 2017). The remainder of the Results section addresses whether a sequence-and-feature model with no external score dependency can deliver clinical utility that meta-stackers cannot.

Feature-group ablation (Figure 1e) supports this framing. Removing structural-context features produced the largest single-group AUROC drop (ΔAUROC = −0.122); removing evolutionary-conservation features produced a modest further drop (−0.021). Critically, removing the externally supplied AlphaFold pLDDT and in-domain annotations alone produced no significant degradation, indicating that the structural information ultimately used by the model is largely internalized in the protein language model itself (Cheng et al., 2023; Jumper et al., 2021). This internalization is the property that makes the model portable to genes with limited functional annotation, and motivates the generalization experiment that follows.

### The thermodynamic head learns transferable variant biophysics on unseen genes

A model that has truly learned variant biophysics should forecast folding consequences on proteins it has never seen during supervised training—a strictly stronger claim than per-variant pathogenicity classification. We partitioned the FoldX ΔΔG supervision set so that ten hearing-loss genes were entirely withheld from training, verified zero gene-level overlap between held-out and training corpora (Figure 2e), and predicted ΔΔG for the five hundred missense variants in the withheld genes.

The aggregate predicted-against-FoldX Spearman correlation was 0.817 (Figure 2a); the per-gene median across the ten genes was 0.826. Against four reference predictors, paired bootstrap yielded deltas in absolute Spearman correlation in favour of HEARS-LM of +0.697 (phyloP-100), +0.547 (ESM-2 zero-shot), +0.454 (AlphaMissense), and +0.423 (REVEL), all with 95 % confidence intervals excluding zero (Figure 2c). The advantage was not driven by a single outlier: HEARS-LM achieved the strongest absolute correlation on every one of the seven evaluable genes with at least twenty held-out variants (ACTG1, CDH23, OTOF, TBC1D24, TECTA, TMC1, WFS1) (Figure 2b). To rule out a regression-target artefact, we examined the distribution of predicted |ΔΔG| stratified by ClinVar classification on the allele-frequency–independent benchmark: pathogenic variants carried substantially larger predicted folding perturbations than benign variants (Mann–Whitney p = 2.2 × 10⁻³⁵; Figure 2d), confirming that the regression target is the physical axis along which clinical interpretation ultimately separates the two groups. Because the held-out genes share zero supervision overlap with the training corpus, what the model has learned during hearing-loss–specific specialization is transferable variant biophysics rather than a memorized lookup of gene-by-mutation effects (Lin et al., 2023; Diaz et al., 2024). The model can therefore be deployed on newly characterized hearing-loss genes for which no training data exist, and the per-residue tolerance maps that follow inherit this transferable biophysical structure rather than re-expressing the distribution of ClinVar labels.

### Per-residue tolerance landscapes outperform AlphaMissense on clinically actionable genes

A predictor with transferable variant biophysics should generate per-residue tolerance maps that respect the clinical landscape, with disease residues concentrated at the most pathogenicity-prone positions. The per-residue formulation also corresponds to how clinicians use predictors—prioritizing residues for follow-up rather than scoring isolated variants—and to the level at which AlphaMissense itself releases saturation predictions (Cheng et al., 2023). For each gene we computed the mean predicted pathogenicity at every position over the nineteen non-reference substitutions and asked whether residues harbouring at least one confident ClinVar pathogenic variant could be retrieved. Thirty-six hearing-loss genes had sufficient ClinVar density to define disease residues; twenty-six supported paired bootstrap comparisons against both AlphaMissense and ESM-2 zero-shot.

HEARS-LM achieved a paired bootstrap-significant ΔAUROC against AlphaMissense in twelve of the twenty-six evaluable genes and against ESM-2 zero-shot in twenty-two, with zero significant losses in either comparison (Figure 3a). The distribution of significant wins is clinically informative (Figure 3f). The largest gains fall on syndromic and complex non-syndromic hearing-loss genes in which disease biology concentrates in specific functional domains that proteome-wide training is poorly positioned to recognize: OTOG (ΔAUROC = +0.606; DFNB18B), SLC4A11 (+0.584; CHED2/DFNB13), LOXHD1 (+0.472; DFNB77), the Usher cluster USH1C/ADGRV1/CDH23/USH2A (ΔAUROC range +0.192 to +0.383; Adato et al., 2005; Mathur & Yang, 2015), ALMS1 (+0.304; Alström), WFS1 (+0.236; Wolfram), OTOF (+0.195; DFNB9, target of the DB-OTO programme NCT05788536; Lv et al., 2024; Bonifert et al., 2022), CHD7 (+0.165; CHARGE), and TECTA (+0.136). These syndromes have historically resisted variant interpretation because their causative proteins carry diverse extracellular and intracellular domains whose hearing-related context is sparsely represented in proteome-wide corpora.

The advantage strengthened on precision-at-K, the metric that most directly mirrors curation triage. Across the thirty-six-gene corpus, mean precision-at-ten was 0.758 for HEARS-LM versus 0.522 for AlphaMissense and 0.425 for ESM-2 zero-shot (Wilcoxon signed-rank p = 3.1 × 10⁻⁶; Figure 3b). Thirteen genes achieved a perfect precision-at-ten of 1.0—every top-ten predicted residue confirmed pathogenic—compared to two for AlphaMissense and two for ESM-2 zero-shot (Figure 3d). On a broader ten-predictor benchmark across forty-two genes, the ordering held: HEARS-LM 0.669, MutPred2 0.494 (Pejaver et al., 2020), DeOgen-2 0.421, METARNN 0.417, PolyPhen-2 0.411 (Adzhubei et al., 2010), REVEL 0.403, AlphaMissense 0.372, PrimateAI 0.372 (Sundaram et al., 2018), ESM-2 zero-shot 0.360, ESM-1b zero-shot 0.116 (Brandes et al., 2023) (Figure 3c). The margin of HEARS-LM over the next-best method is larger than the spread of the remaining nine predictors among themselves. Inheritance stratification (Figure 3e) locates the advantage where it is most needed: median ΔAUROC against AlphaMissense was +0.192 across syndromic genes, +0.154 across autosomal-recessive non-syndromic, +0.012 across autosomal-dominant non-syndromic, and −0.010 on the single X-linked gene. The operational consequence is that HEARS-LM's largest benefit is on syndromic and recessive non-syndromic panels, where diagnostic yield is most constrained by VUS volume (Sloan-Heggen et al., 2016; Oza et al., 2018).

### The model recovers known disease biology in voltage-gated potassium channels

A per-residue benchmark advantage is necessary but not sufficient for biological interpretability. The stronger requirement is that predictions respect what is independently known about disease mechanisms. Pathogenic variants in KCNQ4 (DFNA2A) concentrate in the S1–S4 voltage-sensor and S5–S6 pore domains, with a focal hotspot at the selectivity filter (Coucke et al., 1999; Kubisch et al., 1999). The model received no annotation indicating these are pathogenicity hotspots; if biologically informed, its landscape should rediscover this geometry.

When predicted per-residue pathogenicity was plotted along the 695-residue KCNQ4 protein (Figure 4a), the highest-scoring regions corresponded exactly to the S1, S4, and pore-S6 domains, with the central peak situated over the selectivity filter. The forty-eight curated disease residues mapped predominantly to these regions (residue-level AUROC 0.843; Mann–Whitney p = 1.1 × 10⁻¹⁵; Figure 4b). The pattern was not gene-specific: across six hearing-loss transmembrane proteins, high-confidence predicted pathogenic residues were significantly enriched in transmembrane segments (KCNQ1 47 % vs 20 %, hypergeometric p = 2.4 × 10⁻⁷; KCNQ4 50 % vs 20 %, p = 4.9 × 10⁻⁶; GJB2 55 % vs 37 %, p = 4.5 × 10⁻⁴; Figure 4c). No explicit transmembrane indicator was supplied; the enrichment emerged from the protein language model representation, consistent with the broader observation that such models internalize structural-functional information during pretraining (Lin et al., 2023).

We then tested three-dimensional clustering on AlphaFold structures (Jumper et al., 2021). The mean pairwise Cα distance among high-confidence predicted pathogenic residues was 34.5 Å in KCNQ4 against 62.0 Å under a permutation null (p < 10⁻⁴, ten thousand permutations; Figure 4d); the contrast for KCNQ1 was 40.7 Å versus 54.8 Å (p < 10⁻⁴). The clustering signature was selective rather than universal: in genes whose disease biology does not concentrate in a single structural compartment—CDH23 (Usher variants across twenty-seven extracellular repeats), USH2A, OTOF, CHD7—no significant clustering was observed (p between 0.34 and 0.91). The model thus recovers spatial signatures of disease where biology requires them and does not impose clustering where none should exist. Together with the transmembrane enrichment, this establishes that hearing-loss–specific adaptation produces an internalized representation of disease architecture rather than a memorized table of gene-level priors.

### Predictions align with expert ACMG reasoning and anticipate ClinVar reclassifications

The biological evidence assembled above—transferable thermodynamic generalization, per-residue precision against AlphaMissense, and recovery of established disease geometry—establishes that the model has learned transferable hearing-loss biology. The next question is whether that biology converts into clinical interpretability that expert curators recognize. Two predictions follow: outputs should agree with how the ClinGen Hearing Loss Variant Curation Expert Panel (HL-VCEP) applies the ACMG framework (Richards et al., 2015; DiStefano et al., 2018), and the model should anticipate future ClinVar reclassifications, demonstrating predictive validity beyond retrospective fit.

For the first prediction, we examined agreement with the two ACMG computational-evidence codes most directly comparable to a predictor's output: PP3 (supports pathogenicity) and BP4 (supports benign). Across 167 HL-VCEP-curated variants with both expert annotations and HEARS-LM scores, the mean predicted score was 0.747 for variants assigned PP3 versus 0.497 for those without PP3 (Mann–Whitney p = 1.2 × 10⁻⁶; Figure 5d). The contrast for BP4 was 0.209 versus 0.648 (p = 8.4 × 10⁻⁷). On a sixty-six-variant HL-VCEP held-out subset combining the gene-disjoint and temporal hold-outs, HEARS-LM achieved AUROC 0.901 against expert classifications (Figure 5e), with the gene-disjoint subset reaching 0.971 (n = 21) and the temporal subset 0.912 (n = 43). A focused KCNQ4 analysis flagged five of six residues with HL-VCEP supporting/moderate ACMG codes as high-confidence pathogenic (Figure 5f; odds ratio = 88; hypergeometric p = 4.7 × 10⁻⁶); the single miss was at Gly296, whose own HL-VCEP support is classified as supporting rather than moderate.

The more demanding test is whether the model anticipates decisions experts have not yet made. We used a January 2025 ClinVar snapshot to identify variants then classified as uncertain significance, and an April 2026 snapshot to determine which had been reclassified to pathogenic in the intervening sixteen months. Among 1,153 variants with longitudinal status, twenty-four had been upgraded and 1,129 had remained stable. The mean HEARS-LM score on subsequently upgraded variants was 0.669 versus 0.376 on stable VUS (Mann–Whitney p = 7.4 × 10⁻⁷; Figure 5b), yielding prospective AUROC 0.748 (Figure 5a, 5c), exceeding METARNN (0.732), AlphaMissense (0.722), ESM-2 zero-shot (0.701), and REVEL (0.608) on the identical task. The model thus identifies variants experts will go on to upgrade, even against meta-predictors whose retrospective AUROC is otherwise competitive—the operationally meaningful form of utility in a pipeline whose practical bottleneck is the lag between sequencing and expert classification (Landrum et al., 2020).

### An OTOF-specific resource for the active DB-OTO gene therapy programme

DB-OTO, an investigational adeno-associated viral vector encoding the OTOF transcript, is currently delivered intracochlearly to paediatric patients with biallelic OTOF pathogenic variants (DFNB9; NCT05788536; Lv et al., 2024; Bonifert et al., 2022). The trial requires confident pathogenic classification, and OTOF VUS encountered during panel sequencing constitute a recurring bottleneck for patient eligibility (Gallego-Bartolomé & Salt, 2023). We generated a per-residue tolerance landscape for the 1,997-residue OTOF protein (Figure 6a) and asked how well it recovers the 108 OTOF residues with at least one confident ClinVar pathogenic variant. Residue-level AUROC was 0.950, compared to 0.752 for AlphaMissense and 0.626 for ESM-2 zero-shot (paired ΔAUROC = +0.198 and +0.324; Figure 6b). Mapping the profile onto the OTOF domain architecture—six tandem C2 domains and a single transmembrane domain—the highest-scoring residues concentrate in the C2D and C2E domains, which mediate the calcium-dependent membrane interaction essential for synaptic vesicle fusion at hair-cell ribbon synapses. Sixteen additional high-confidence VUS in these functional C2 domains were flagged as candidates for elevated clinical review.

Extending the procedure to the twelve syndromic hearing-loss genes with the largest VUS burdens in ClinVar (Figure 6c), the model identified 227 high-confidence reclassification candidates at threshold 0.8. The largest contributions came from WFS1 (fifty-nine of 471 VUS, 13 %), USH2A (fifty of 1,783, 3 %), CDH23 (forty-three of 1,216, 4 %), and LOXHD1 (twenty-two of 423, 5 %). Partitioning by proximity to known ClinVar pathogenic residues (Figure 6d), 181 candidates (80 %) sit at positions where at least one other pathogenic variant is already deposited, 26 (11 %) at positions within five amino acids of one (within-hotspot extension), and 20 (9 %) at ultra-novel positions more than five amino acids from any known pathogenic site. The ultra-novel candidates, distributed across nine syndromic genes including USH2A, CDH23, WFS1, LOXHD1, CHD7, ADGRV1, and TECTA (Figure 6e), constitute testable biological hypotheses for prospective experimental follow-up: residues that no other reasoning channel has yet identified.

The cumulative evidence supports a coherent interpretation. A biology-aware model that integrates protein language representation with hearing-loss feature context learns transferable variant biophysics on genes never seen during supervision, produces per-residue tolerance maps that outperform AlphaMissense on the most clinically actionable syndromic genes, recovers the spatial signature of disease in voltage-gated channels while declining to impose clustering where biology does not require it, aligns with ACMG expert reasoning at significance below 10⁻⁶, anticipates ClinVar reclassifications sixteen months in advance of expert decisions, and produces a tractable OTOF resource for the active DB-OTO gene-therapy programme. In a Mendelian-disease setting characterized by locus heterogeneity and a persistent VUS bottleneck, biology-aware specialization is more productive than further pursuit of per-variant AUROC alone.

## Discussion

The HHL community has lived for several years with a paradox: per-variant pathogenicity prediction has improved markedly, yet variants of uncertain significance continue to dominate clinical reports in hearing-loss panels. We reframed the prediction task accordingly. Rather than competing with general-purpose meta-stackers on the proteome-wide per-variant AUROC at which they are explicitly optimized, we asked whether a smaller biology-specific objective—learn transferable HHL protein biophysics and produce per-residue maps that align with expert reasoning—could serve clinicians better in the moments when they need it most. The evidence above suggests it can. Three findings carry the argument: the auxiliary thermodynamic head generalizes to unseen genes (per-gene median Spearman ρ = 0.826), establishing transferable biophysics rather than memorized gene-by-mutation effects; the model independently recovers spatial signatures of disease in voltage-gated channels, recapitulating transmembrane and pore concentration without explicit instruction; and the model anticipates ClinVar reclassifications more than a year before experts issue them.

We position the contribution carefully relative to two reference classes of predictors. AlphaMissense (Cheng et al., 2023) remains the leading single-model proteome-wide predictor and provides excellent variant-level scores on genes whose biology is well-represented in its training population. On the most extensively studied syndromic HHL genes—the Usher cluster, LOXHD1, OTOF—we improve substantially over AlphaMissense in both per-residue AUROC (ΔAUROC up to +0.606 in OTOG) and clinical decision-support precision (36 % perfect precision-at-ten versus 6 % for AlphaMissense and ESM-2 zero-shot). Meta-stacking predictors such as METARNN and BAYESDEL (Li et al., 2022; Feng, 2017) achieve higher absolute per-variant AUROC on conventional ClinVar splits, but this advantage rests on twenty or more upstream predictor scores that restrict coverage to variants for which every upstream tool has already returned a score. HEARS-LM operates on protein sequence alone and is therefore deployable on novel-gene variants the moment a panel is sequenced. The two approaches are complementary: meta-stackers serve at the variant-list triage step, while a hearing-loss–specialized model is more useful at the per-residue and per-gene prioritization step that immediately precedes clinical reasoning.

The prospective alignment with ClinGen HL-VCEP expert decisions carries direct practical implications. The HL-VCEP applies PP3 and BP4 evidence codes manually under conservative interpretation rules (DiStefano et al., 2018), and the choice of predictor for those codes has been an ongoing topic of discussion. HEARS-LM's mean predicted pathogenicity differs by 0.25 between PP3 and no-PP3 variants and by 0.44 between BP4 and no-BP4 variants, with Mann–Whitney p values below 10⁻⁶ in both cases—effect sizes large enough to support consideration of the model as a domain-specific PP3/BP4 evidence source, although any such adoption would require independent prospective validation by the panel and calibration to its decision thresholds. The OTOF resource is, in our view, the most immediately actionable output. The DB-OTO programme is recruiting patients with biallelic OTOF pathogenic variants, and panel sequencing in candidate patients regularly returns OTOF variants that fall short of pathogenic classification under conservative ACMG application, with corresponding implications for audiologic management including cochlear implantation (Eppsteiner et al., 2012). The 1,997-residue OTOF tolerance map released here (residue-level AUROC 0.950) can be consulted alongside expert review to prioritize trial-relevant variants, and the methodology is extensible to other gene-therapy candidate genes that the HHL community is actively pursuing, including STRC and SLC26A4.

Several limitations of this work should be acknowledged. The thermodynamic supervision is restricted to single-point folding free-energy estimates from a single force field; although the predicted ΔΔG values generalize convincingly, the ground truth itself is approximate, and future work could integrate experimental thermal stability data (Niesen et al., 2007). The mechanism head exhibits limited per-variant generalization (held-out AUROC of 0.506 for the loss-of-function subclass), reflecting the small and gene-level character of available mechanism training labels; we therefore report it as a secondary diagnostic output rather than a clinical-decision channel. The high-confidence reclassification catalogue is dominated by within-hotspot variants (91 % within five residues of a known pathogenic position), and although the 9 % ultra-novel fraction provides testable biological hypotheses, the catalogue cannot replace orthogonal functional or family-segregation evidence. Finally, we did not analyze splicing, structural, or non-coding regulatory variants, all of which contribute meaningfully to the HHL VUS burden (Avraham et al., 2022; Stenson et al., 2017).

In closing, the framework introduced here—domain specialization of a protein language model through low-rank adaptation, joint training with a thermodynamic auxiliary task, and evaluation against per-residue and prospective clinical criteria rather than per-variant AUROC alone—offers a generalizable template for Mendelian-disease variant interpretation. Hearing loss is a particularly favorable test case because its causal genes span an unusual diversity of biophysical functions in a small anatomical compartment, but the same principles should apply to inherited cardiomyopathies, retinal dystrophies, and other Mendelian disease categories in which expert curation panels bear the brunt of the variant-classification burden. We anticipate the resources released here and the methodological pattern they exemplify will be useful both to the HHL clinical community in the near term and to the broader Mendelian-disease genomics community over time.
